Biography

I have always being fascinated by how things work, and this led me to study molecular biology at University. I then pursued a PhD in cell biology, to study how neural stem cells divide asymmetrically in the brain of a fruit fly. However, during my PhD I realized I wanted to do something more fast paced and that would allow me to keep in mind the big picture. I also became invested in the environmental cause, the green energy transition and sustainable nutrition. I started learning data science and coding in Python because I like to ask questions and solving problems, all things graphs and color palettes.

PhD project

Division is one of the most important events in the life of a cell, which ensures that the genetic material and the entire set of cellular components segregate in the correct way between the two daughter cells. To divide, cells have to undergo profound shape changes in a timely manner. During my PhD I studied how this is regulated using the neuronal stem cells of the fruit fly larval brain. These cells are unusual in that they divide asymmetrically to produce a large and a small daughter cell with different fates. This model system is particularly interesting because theis type of cells face additional challenges in carrying out a robust division without mistakes, and by studying them we can learn new mechanisms that are true for animal cell division in general.
